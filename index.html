PROJECT IDEAS

Appliance Control App

An IT project idea that I’ve come up with is a mobile application that alarms the user, who has left their home, whenever a light is still on, a door is unlocked or a stove is still on. The app should go beyond that and have a function on the app that allows the user to turn the lights and stove off and lock or close any doors. Overtime, the project will have additional features for different parts of the home. The project will also include the use of specific lights, stoves and doors that will be capable of syncing with the app. As well as special made stoves, lights and doors that are designed specifically for the app to be able to sync.

In 2018, CFA reported 1588 preventable house fires in Victoria alone. With the use of the application, the alarm or notification feature will warn the homeowner that their stove is still on and allows them to turn it off through the app. This will prevent many house fires and slowly reduce the rates whether in Victoria alone or hopefully, all around the world. The rates of robbery in Australia will also decrease from a recorded number of 225,900 (Budget Direct, 2017) incidents. By simply alerting the homeowner a door is unlocked or open, they can either quickly return home or control from the app, this will easily prevent strangers from breaking into the home. Using home automation software for this project will end or at least lessen these occurrences for everyday people.

The app will firstly have a feature that allows users to put in their location so that when they leave their home, the app will automatically turn on and start scanning once the user is at least 100m away from their home. This will allow the user to easily turn back around and close or lock any doors the app has detected opened or turn off any lights or the stove. Once a notification or alarm appears, there is an option to say there are other people home or allows you to ignore it. There will be alerts every 10 minutes if the user does not respond to the alert. The app will run without requiring an Internet connection so that users will get an alert wherever they may be even if they are not connected to wifi. Similar to the alarm app on the iPhone, alarms will go off and make sound even if the phone is on silent, I want the app to have this feature so there is no risk of the user getting a stove on warning and it being too late. After detecting a risk, and identifying the user is far away from their house, the app will allow the user to turn off their stove or light or lock their door simply by pressing a button. A message will pop up saying it had been successful to validate and lessen the user’s worry. An alternative and an upgrade to purchasing accordant appliances will be to have a small extension that can connect to all the appliances in the home and can be controlled by the app.

To produce this application, multiple software programs are required. Firstly, programs like Appery .io will allow me to build the mobile app itself for both Android and iOS. as well as home automation software like Home Assistant or HomeKit. These home automation softwares are used in similar apps such as Google Home which inspired this project. At the moment, HomeKit focuses on lightbulb control through smartphones, this software will be similar to the one needed for the project as turning lights off an on is part of the app. A software program will also be needed for the detection aspect of the app, to identify when a stove is on or a door is unlocked.

There are many skills needed to for the project to be as successful as it can be. Focusing on the overall appearance and management of the app, the main skills required will be immense product management skills so that the app will always be in line with the user’s needs and wants as the apps main goal is to assist the user as best as possible, as well as design skills so it attracts both users and potential users also making sure it is easy for everyone to use, regardless of age or disadvantages. The syncing process should be made easy with only a few clicks through bluetooth and navigation of the app so that users come to rely on the app. Running the app will need clean and exceptional coding skills and knowledge of Java, HTML, C++, CCS and UI framework to list a few. Also data skills to collect the end results and work with the results to improve on the app.

It is ideal that majority of the population invest in the app or rely on it as the problems it aims to tackle are negatively life changing, yet preventable. Therefore, if the project is successful in the number of users and how accurate the app runs, house fires from stoves left on unattended and home invasions will decrease immensely However, some drawbacks to this project may be that users end up relying too much on the app and lose their cautiousness when leaving the house and following routine of checking stoves and locking doors, instead they may walk out and expect an alarm or notification to go off on their phone. It may be a timely process to sync every light in the house or purchase new ones compatible with the app. The cost of the instalment of the specific stoves, lights and doors will be quite an investment for some or an unnecessary cost, especially for those who live in older homes that will need the full installation. Whereas, in newer homes, those will already be built in on request. To tackle the cost problem, there may be free installation around the neighbourhood to get users familiar with the app.
